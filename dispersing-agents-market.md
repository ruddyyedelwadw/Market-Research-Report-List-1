<p><p>Dispersing agents are used to stabilize particles in a solution by preventing them from clumping together. There are three main types of dispersing agents on the market - anionic, cationic, and others. Anionic dispersing agents have a negative charge and are commonly used in industries such as food, pharmaceuticals, and ceramics. Cationic dispersing agents have a positive charge and are often used in personal care products and water treatment. Other dispersing agents include nonionic and amphoteric types that cater to specific industry needs.</p></p>

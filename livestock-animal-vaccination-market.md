<p><p>The livestock animal vaccination market types can be categorized into three main groups. Firstly, live attenuated vaccines involve the use of weakened forms of the disease-causing pathogens to stimulate an immune response in animals. Secondly, inactivated vaccines consist of killed pathogens that are incapable of causing the disease but can still provoke an immune response. Lastly, other types of vaccines in this market include subunit vaccines, DNA vaccines, and recombinant vaccines that employ various techniques to create immunity against specific livestock diseases. Each type has its unique mechanism and effectiveness in protecting animals from infectious diseases.</p></p>

<p><p>Silicon-based anode materials for Li-ion batteries typically fall into two main categories: SiO/C and Si/C. SiO/C anodes consist of silicon oxide nanoparticles embedded in a carbon matrix, offering improved conductivity and structural stability. On the other hand, Si/C anodes feature silicon particles dispersed in a carbon matrix, providing higher energy density and cycling stability. Both types have shown promising results in enhancing the performance of Li-ion batteries, making them attractive options for the next generation of energy storage devices.</p></p>

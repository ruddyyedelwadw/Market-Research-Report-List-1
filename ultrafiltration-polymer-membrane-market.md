<p><p>Ultrafiltration polymer membranes are divided into two main types: inorganic membranes and organic membranes. Inorganic membranes are typically made from materials like ceramics or metals, offering higher durability and resistance to harsh chemicals. On the other hand, organic membranes are made from polymers like polyethersulfone or polyvinylidene fluoride, providing better flexibility and cost-effectiveness. Both types of membranes are widely used in various industries for separating particles and molecules based on size through ultrafiltration processes.</p></p>

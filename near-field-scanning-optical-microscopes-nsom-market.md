<p><p>Near-Field Scanning Optical Microscopes (NSOM) are a type of microscopy technique that enables nanoscale resolution imaging by using a sharp probe to scan the sample surface at close proximity. This allows for the observation of features smaller than the diffraction limit of traditional optical microscopes. NSOM has applications in various fields such as nanotechnology, materials science, and biological research.</p><p>The NSOM market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 10.1% during the forecast period. The increasing demand for high-resolution imaging techniques for nanoscale research and development is driving the growth of the NSOM market. Furthermore, advancements in technology, such as the development of new probes and improved signal detection methods, are also contributing to the market growth.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the NSOM market is the integration of NSOM with other imaging techniques, such as atomic force microscopy (AFM) and scanning electron microscopy (SEM), to provide complementary information about the sample surface. This trend is expected to continue as researchers seek to obtain a more comprehensive understanding of nanoscale structures and properties.</p></p>

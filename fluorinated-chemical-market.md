<p><p>Fluorinated chemicals are commonly divided into two main categories - inorganic and organic. Inorganic fluorinated chemicals usually contain ions like fluoride, fluorosilicate, or hexafluorosilicate and are widely used in industries such as manufacturing, water treatment, and electronics. On the other hand, organic fluorinated chemicals contain carbon-fluorine bonds and are commonly found in products like pharmaceuticals, agrochemicals, and surfactants. Both types of fluorinated chemicals play crucial roles in various industries due to their unique properties and applications.</p></p>

<p><p>Micro displays are small screens used in devices such as smartphones, smartwatches, and AR/VR headsets. There are four main types of micro display technologies: OLED, LCoS, DLP, and LCD. </p><p>OLED displays use organic compounds to emit light, providing high contrast ratios and color accuracy. LCoS (Liquid Crystal on Silicon) displays use liquid crystals to modulate light. DLP (Digital Light Processing) displays use digital micromirrors to reflect light onto the screen. LCD (Liquid Crystal Display) screens use liquid crystals to block or allow light to pass through, offering high resolution and brightness.</p></p>

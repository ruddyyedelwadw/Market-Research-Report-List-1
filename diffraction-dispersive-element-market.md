<p><ul><li>Ruled Dispersive Element</li><li>Holographic Dispersive Element</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Diffraction Dispersive Elements are used in spectrometers for separating light into its different wavelengths. Ruled Dispersive Elements have grooves or lines ruled onto a surface to diffract light, while Holographic Dispersive Elements use holographic technology to achieve the same result. Ruled elements are more common and cost-effective, while holographic elements offer better performance and design flexibility. Both types play crucial roles in various industries such as astronomy, chemistry, and physics for analyzing light spectra and identifying materials.</p></p>

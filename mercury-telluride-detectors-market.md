<p><p>Mercury telluride detectors are commonly classified into two types based on their operating principle: photovoltaic and photoconductive. Photovoltaic detectors generate a voltage when exposed to infrared radiation, making them ideal for low-light applications. On the other hand, photoconductive detectors measure changes in conductivity when light is absorbed, offering higher sensitivity. Both types of detectors play a crucial role in various industries such as defense, aerospace, and scientific research where precise detection of infrared radiation is required.</p></p>

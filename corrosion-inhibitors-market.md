<p><p>Corrosion inhibitors are chemicals that are added to an environment in order to prevent or reduce the rate of corrosion of metals. There are two main types of corrosion inhibitors: organic and inorganic. Organic corrosion inhibitors are derived from carbon-based compounds and are often used in industries such as oil and gas, water treatment, and automotive. Inorganic corrosion inhibitors, on the other hand, are generally metal-based compounds that form protective coatings on the surface of metals to prevent corrosion. Both types of inhibitors play a crucial role in protecting metal infrastructure and equipment from degradation.</p></p>

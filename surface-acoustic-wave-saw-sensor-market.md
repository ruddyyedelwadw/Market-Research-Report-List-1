<p><p>Surface Acoustic Wave (SAW) sensors are categorized into two main types: resonators and delay lines. Resonators operate by measuring the frequency changes in the wave as it interacts with the target substance, providing precise detection capabilities. On the other hand, delay lines work by measuring the time it takes for the wave to travel from the input to the output, offering high sensitivity and accuracy. Both resonators and delay lines play a crucial role in various industries such as automotive, healthcare, and defense.</p></p>

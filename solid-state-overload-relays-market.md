<p><p>Solid-State Overload Relays are used to protect motors from overcurrent conditions. There are two main types: Thermal Overload Relay and Magnetic Overload Relay. Thermal Overload Relays use the heating effect of current to trip the relay, while Magnetic Overload Relays use the magnetic field generated by current to trip the relay. Both types offer reliable protection for motors and are widely used in industrial applications. The market for these relays is driven by the need for efficient and safe motor protection in various industries.</p></p>
